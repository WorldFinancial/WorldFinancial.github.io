Alibaba and Tencent look cheap, but can Chinese stocks be bought now?

Created2022-03-26|Updated2023-08-03|Internet
|Post View:

INVESTING.com - China’s big tech stocks appear to be showing signs of recovery after taking a number of hits over the past year. Last week, Chinese regulators revealed their intention to support financial stability, sending the Nasdaq Golden Dragon China Index up 33 percent in a single day, its biggest daily gain ever.

Among them was e-commerce giant Alibaba (NYSE:BABA) (BABA), which has gained 52.7 per cent since then. The stock closed at $117.24 on Wednesday.

At a special meeting of the Financial Stability Development Committee of the Chinese State Council earlier this month, Xinhua issued a circular stating, “Regarding Chinese stocks, the regulators of China and the United States have maintained good communication and have made positive progress, and are working to form a concrete cooperation plan. The Chinese government continues to support all kinds of enterprises to list abroad.”

However, the series of good news has not completely removed the macroeconomic headwinds that Chinese stocks are still facing, with one of the biggest concerns being the Russia-Ukraine geopolitical conflict.

Last week, JP Morgan downgraded 28 Chinese internet stocks, including Alibaba and Tencent Holdings, which the bank said were “uninvestable” for the next six to 12 months due to rising geopolitical and macro risks.

Another uncertainty that will continue to weigh on Chinese stocks is the previously released US Foreign Company Accountability Act and rules. The implementation of this law and rules will have a direct impact on the survival of Chinese stocks. If the Chinese stocks continue to fail to meet the audit requirements of the US regulators, the ultimate fate will be delisting. The list already includes companies such as Yum China, Sina Weibo and BGNE.

In addition, the progress of the current epidemic in China is a key point for investors in Chinese stocks to keep a close eye on.

However, it is not all bad news.

Firstly after a year of consolidation, there are signs that the regulatory scrutiny of the tech sector in terms of anti-monopoly and data protection has come to an end and these giants are once again seeing growth.

One of them, Alibaba, told investors in its earnings report last month that it would now prioritise user retention over acquisitions. It’s a major shift for a company that has become China’s biggest tech company by pursuing sustained growth. The company’s latest quarterly earnings report showed that its revenue growth for the quarter was the slowest since it went public.

However, Alibaba announced an expanded share buyback programme this week, which boosted its US-listed stock.

Meanwhile, Tencent also released its latest quarterly earnings report on Wednesday. The company posted its slowest quarterly growth rate on record, hit by weak online advertising and gaming revenue. Compared to its peak in 2021, Tencent has lost more than US$470 billion.

Overall, investing in the Chinese stock market remains a high-risk bet, given the multiple risks facing investors in Chinese stocks. While there are signs that the pressure on these tech giants has eased, it is hard to imagine that the road to rapid expansion will remain thorny for these companies in the future. Therefore, in our view, it is best for investors to remain on the sidelines.
