These two cryptocurrencies may replace bitcoin and ethereum

Created2022-03-27|Updated2023-08-03|Internet
|Post View:

The cryptocurrency asset class has been on a bumpy ride. Since the quiet emergence of BTC at 5 cents each, cryptocurrencies have seen an explosion in value. Despite the fact that the digital currency’s price trend has been unpromising since November 2021, Bitcoin is currently trading at $43,011 and its appreciation since its launch has been astounding.

Shortly afterwards Ether was born, the two highest market capitalisation tokens are the progenitors of other tokens, and the number of new tokens is increasing daily. The tenth largest cryptocurrency in this asset class by market capitalisation is already worth over $23.1 billion.

Both Cardano and Solana are among the high-value digital currencies. Although ADA and SOL have corrected from their 2021 highs, they are still attracting a lot of attention.

Pressure on leading cryptocurrencies continues, but signs of a rebound are already visible

Bitcoin and Ether prices are closer to their January 24 lows than their November 10, 2021 highs.

Near-monthly Ether futures on March 24 were priced at $3,053.50, $816.50 above the January 24 low and $1,848 below the November 10 high.

Meanwhile, bitcoin and ethereum have been moving lower on their highs but higher on their lows, forming a wedge. The leading cryptocurrencies are building up energy for a rally. In terms of long-term trends, the likelihood of higher prices is high.

Bitcoin and ethereum are the crypto market leaders, with some well-capitalised tokens following close behind.

Cardano: Proof-of-stake cryptocurrency

Cardano is a public blockchain platform. It is open source and decentralised, and uses a proof-of-stake protocol that requires less energy than a proof-of-work protocol. Cardano uses its native cryptocurrency ADA to facilitate peer-to-peer transactions.

Cardano was founded in 2015 by Charles Hoskinson, co-founder of Ether. On 24 March, ADA became the seventh largest cryptocurrency with a market cap of over $38.5 billion.

Solana - the challenger to Ether

Solana is a public platform for blockchain with smart contract functionality. Solana’s local cryptocurrency is SOL, which Solana claims offers faster transaction times and lower costs compared to its main competitor Ether.

On March 24, SOL was the ninth-ranked cryptocurrency.

SOL has a market cap of $97.32, or over $31 billion.

SOL started trading at 78.0 cents per token in April 2020.

SOL chart, source: CoinMarketCapSOL chart, source: CoinMarketCap
SOL had reached a high of $258.93 in November 2021. Over the past few months, the price of SOL has corrected significantly. Like ADA, it offers traders and investors plenty of liquidity and ranks among the top asset classes.

When ADA and SOL break the current wedge pattern, they could follow Bitcoin and Ether higher. ADA and SOL will likely become alternatives to these two top cryptocurrencies.

However, the greater the reward, the higher the risk. Digital currencies are all high-risk, high-reward assets and investors need to manage their risk well.
