As Tencent enters winter, when will spring arrive?

Created2022-03-27|Updated2023-08-03|Internet
|Post View:

On 23 March 2022, Chinese internet giant Tencent released its latest earnings report which was unexpectedly weak, with the lowest net profit increase in a decade. Overnight, it seems the days of Tencent making money lying down are gone.

Tencent Holdings ADR (OTC:TCEHY) plunged more than 5 percent that night, and Hong Kong shares of Tencent Holdings (HK:0700) fell 5.91 percent on Thursday.

Tencent’s results “thunder”

In terms of revenue, Tencent’s annual revenue for 2021 was $560.118 billion, up 16% year-on-year, but Q4 revenue was $144.188 billion, up just 8% year-on-year, recording the slowest single-quarter growth rate since its IPO.

Tencent’s net profit jumped 60% in Q4 2021, but this was mainly due to the inclusion of a one-off income of $78 billion from the reduction of its holding in Jingdong. In fact, under non-IFRS Non-IFRS, Tencent’s net profit fell by 25%, the second consecutive quarter of negative single-quarter net profit growth, following a 2% drop in the third quarter.

For the full year of 2021, Tencent’s total revenue reached 560.12 billion yuan, up 16% year-on-year; net of investments including a gain of 78.2 billion yuan from the disposal of its stake in Jingdong, net profit under Non-IFRS was 123.79 billion yuan, up just 1% year-on-year, the lowest increase in nearly a decade, bearing in mind that the figure for 2020 is 30%!

A cold wave for the gaming industry

Over the past few years, Tencent has been unable to shake off its “gaming company” label, despite being a very diversified company. Tencent’s gaming business has been much criticised, and “King of Glory”, which contributes the most to Tencent’s gaming revenue, has been described by the media as a “spiritual opiate” and a “pesticide”.

In recent years, the state has stepped up its efforts to regulate the gaming industry. Last year, the Central Office and the State Office issued the Opinions on Further Reducing the Burden of Homework and Off-Campus Training for Students at the Compulsory Education Stage, which called for guiding students to use electronic products reasonably, controlling the length of use and preventing Internet addiction.

At the same time, the state has also reduced the issuance of game royalty numbers. The game license number is a necessary element for a game to be commercialised, and only when the license number application is completed can it be opened for users to top up and pay. In other words, a game can only be profitable if it has a license number.

According to public information, the last list of games approved by the State Press and Publication Administration was published on 22 July 2021, after which there were rumours that new regulations on license numbers would be implemented, but no new license numbers have been issued for eight months now. The gaming industry has long since entered a cold winter.

The company’s president, Liu Keping, said on the call that he expected to see more new regulatory requirements introduced in the future, but that they would be incremental. He expressed confidence that regulators remain supportive of the gaming industry and have prepared a series of games awaiting approval to resume.

Tencent’s announcement said that in terms of limiting the number of hours played and spent by minors, the company’s total hours played by minors in all four seasons of 2021 decreased by 88% year-on-year, representing 0.9% of the total hours played in the local market. Total underage game sales decreased by 73% year-on-year, accounting for 1.5% of total game sales in the local market.

Tencent’s change

Tencent has always been one of the largest gaming companies in the world.

The delay in issuing a license for the games industry is not necessarily all bad news for the top companies, at least not in terms of new competitors. Tencent’s domestic gaming revenue for the fourth quarter was RMB 29.6 billion (1% domestic year-on-year) and overseas revenue was RMB 13.2 billion (32% overseas year-on-year), for a total revenue of RMB 42.8 billion, up 9.46% year-on-year. This year-on-year growth rate is slightly better than the previous quarter and has improved quarter-on-quarter since Q2, starting to gradually shake off the high base impact of the epidemic and the impact of anti-addiction.

King’s Glory still firmly dominates the top spot in game revenue, and the overseas game market is growing at a better rate than the domestic one, which is very promising.

In terms of revenue structure, Tencent’s financial technology and enterprise services business generated revenue of 48 billion yuan in the fourth quarter, up 25%. For the first time, Tencent’s To B business overtook online games to become Tencent’s top revenue source.

It is worth noting, however, that although the fintech and enterprise services section surpassed the gaming business in terms of revenue volume, it will still take time to catch up in terms of profits. In the case of Tencent Cloud, for example, it is still in a period of huge investment and huge losses will continue. In particular, the competitive price war in the industry is becoming increasingly fierce, and AliCloud, which is the number one market share in China, also just announced that it had achieved break-even for the first time not long ago.

Share buyback

China’s internet majors are cutting back on foreign investment due to anti-monopoly and other regulatory issues. As a result, Tencent has chosen to buy back its own shares.

Tencent said it would buy back its own shares first than invest in other companies when it believes its share price has reached a very attractive point. Tencent has bought back its shares 10 times so far this year, spending HK$160 million, according to the data.

It is indeed a small amount compared to Alibaba’s (NYSE:BABA) astronomical buyback programme of US$25 billion, but it does show management’s confidence in the company. The market doesn’t seem to be buying it, however, as Tencent’s share price has fallen almost 20 percent so far this year.

Fair value

According to InvestingPro’s model, Tencent has a fair value of HK$591.86, 61.7% above the current share price. Wall Street analysts have given an average target price of HK$560, 53% above the current price, with a target range of HK$265 to HK$837.

In terms of financial health, Tencent also performed very well with an overall score of 4 out of 5.

Epilogue

The gaming industry, and indeed the internet industry as a whole, may have entered a cold winter. Can an already so cold winter get any colder?

I believe that regulation is not meant to wipe out the internet industry, but rather to have a healthy and smooth development of this industry. In the long run, this will help the giants to settle down and focus on innovation, and to deepen and empower the real economy to achieve a better integration of the virtual and real economies.

Tencent has always been a very good company. The current valuation is undoubtedly undervalued, but confidence in the market has not yet been fully restored and the valuation may not yet be killed to the bottom.

Winter is here, can spring be far behind?
