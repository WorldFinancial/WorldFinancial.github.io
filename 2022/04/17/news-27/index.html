Explaining the 2022 Miami Bitcoin Conference

Created2022-04-17|Updated2023-08-03|Internet
|Post View:

On April 6, 2022, the world-renowned Bitcoin Conference took place as scheduled in Miami, Florida, USA. The conference, organized by Nashville-based BTC Media, focuses on macro conversations ranging from how traditional financial systems, institutions and infrastructure integrate with Bitcoin to detailed explorations of mining to crypto industry regulatory pain points, predicted policies that guide crypto companies on the cost of compliance and how the crypto industry can thrive in compliance.

The conference runs until the official end on April 9 and includes 1 day of industry days, 2 days of major conference days and 1 day of festivals. There are 35,000 attendees at the conference, including government figures, financial giants, tech elites, tech gurus and Bitcoin mining stakeholders from around the world. In addition, there were over 100 artists confirmed to attend the conference.

Next, take a look at what happened at the conference.

Miami Mayor: The Next US President Must Be a Pro-Bitcoin Candidate
Miami Mayor Francis Suarez opened “Bitcoin 2022” with a speech advocating for the election of pro-Bitcoin officials. “The next president of the United States must be a pro-Bitcoin candidate,” he said. Suarez said he wants to articulate a “national vision” related to crypto, which he called “Bitcoin America Vision 2024. In addition to pushing for the election of pro-bitcoin candidates who can shape a favorable regulatory environment, Suarez called for the integration of bitcoin into “every part of our society,” including the use of cryptocurrency to purchase everyday items.

Star Investor Kevin O’Leary: Good Policies Will Open Doors for Capital in Cryptocurrency
Crypto investor and Shark Tank star Kevin O’Leary made his support for policy clear during his keynote address at the Bitcoin 2022 conference in Miami. o’Leary said that with meaningful regulation in place, a faucet of capital will flood into the sector in unprecedented ways. According to O’Leary, this includes becoming the 12th sector in the S&P 500 index over the next decade. He points to a bill that Bitcoin-friendly Senator Cynthia Lummis has touted, recently announcing that she is a co-sponsor of Senator Kirsten Gillibrand. The specifics of the bill have yet to come to light, but Lummis and Gillibrand said during a recent panel discussion that the bill would create a broad regulatory framework for cryptocurrencies.

Cash App Allows Users to Seamlessly Convert Paychecks to Bitcoin
Cash App, the mobile payments platform owned by Block (formerly known as Square), announced at the conference the launch of its new product Paid in Bitcoin, which allows users with Cash Cards to use a portion of their paychecks directly to automatically invest in bitcoin. It also said that in the next few weeks, Cash App will also launch more features, including Round Ups and more. Earlier this year, Cash App announced integration with the Bitcoin Lightning Network, designed to enable users to pay for services or goods instantly and inexpensively using Bitcoin. In the near future, Cash App US users (excluding New York State) will officially be able to use this feature.

Top Silicon Valley Investor Peter Thiel Predicts Bitcoin Price Will Rise 100x in the Future
Peter Thiel, a top Silicon Valley investor, predicted that the price of Bitcoin will rise 100 times in the future, while explaining the difference between cryptocurrencies and fiat currencies in his keynote speech at the 2022 Bitcoin Conference. Peter Thiel also denounced Warren Buffett and JP Morgan CEO Jamie Dimon for their previous skepticism of Bitcoin.

Honduran Island, Portuguese Autonomous Region, Mexico Intend to Make Bitcoin Legal Tender
Former Blockstream Chief Strategy Officer Samson Mow says the Honduran island of Roatán and the Portuguese autonomous region of Madeira have confirmed they will adopt bitcoin as legal tender, while Mexican Senator Indira Kempis has submitted legislation to make bitcoin legal tender.

Robinhood Plans to Use Lightning Network for Bitcoin Trading
Stock and cryptocurrency trading platform Robinhood announced plans at the Bitcoin 2022 conference to add the Bitcoin Lightning Network to its platform to speed up BTC transactions. robinhood also revealed that qualified users on its beta wallet waiting list can already send and receive cryptocurrencies via the crypto wallet feature.

The Lightning Network is a 2-tier network built on top of Bitcoin that enables faster, lower-cost transfers. robinhood unveiled plans to develop a crypto wallet six months ago. Now with this feature, Robinhood will also join the growing number of tech and financial companies using the Lightning Network for bitcoin transactions. The Robinhood crypto wallet, which currently has a waiting list of more than 2 million people, was released in beta in January, allowing some users to transfer bitcoin, ethereum and dogcoin.

U.S. Bitcoin Mining Giant Is Looking to Expand to the Middle East and Europe
“We’re looking at energy resource and capital-rich regions like the Middle East and Scandinavia to expand our business,” said Mike Levitt, CEO of Austin, Texas-based mining company Core Scientific, at the Bitcoin 2022 conference in Miami.

Fred Thiel, CEO of Marathon Digital, the largest publicly traded bitcoin miner in North America, also said at the conference that he is also looking at the Middle East, saying, “The Middle East is a very interesting place because they have asymmetries in terms of power and energy.” The U.S. bitcoin mining giant is looking to Scandinavia, the Middle East and other regions to expand its energy-intensive, loosely regulated sector.

Mexican Billionaire Ricardo Salinas: 60% of Portfolio in Bitcoin-Related Assets
Ricardo Salinas Pliego, Mexico’s third richest man with a net worth of $13.6 billion, said during the Bit2022 conference that 60 percent of his liquid portfolio is in bitcoin. salinas said, In the liquid portfolio, I have 60 percent in bitcoin and bitcoin stocks. 40 percent is invested in hard asset classes such as oil, natural gas and gold mining. He believes bonds are the worst asset to invest in, and if you put $100 in, you’re lucky to get $100 back. In a discussion about a 60/40 traditional portfolio allocation, it was argued that 10% of that $200 trillion could go to bitcoin. That would be $20 trillion, turning Bitcoin into $1 million each. Against this backdrop, Salinas said he invests in bitcoin and hard assets. He did not specify the amount of his liquid portfolio, but the disclosure makes him the first billionaire to announce that he is invested almost entirely in bitcoin.

Tesla Solar Technology to Power Bitcoin Mining Facility Built by Block and Blockstream Partnership
Blockstream co-founder and CEO Adam Back said at the Bitcoin 2022 conference that Tesla’s 3.8 megawatt solar photovoltaic array and 12 megawatt-hour Megapack will power the facility and will prove that 100 percent renewable energy can be used for large-scale bitcoin mining.

We are excited to start building this facility using Tesla Solar and Megapack,” Adam Back said in a press release. This is a step towards proving our argument that bitcoin mining can fund a zero-emissions electricity infrastructure and build economic growth for the future.”

With the conclusion of the Sound Money festival, Bitcoin 2022 has officially come to a close. The conference was both a revolution and a four-day pilgrimage for attendees. It brought together a wide range of Bitcoin ecosystem companies, projects and industry elites, providing participants with the opportunity to interact and learn, while it was also a great opportunity for companies to promote their existing projects.

According to the feedback from the participants of the conference, “The Miami Bitcoin Conference has always been a blockchain industry event, where the top companies and teams in the industry come together to discuss the future prospects of the industry and the problems that need to be solved today, which helps to promote the rapid development of the industry as a whole. We also look forward to learning different operation models and technological innovations from the top companies, teams in the blockchain industry here to create a better transaction experience for users.”
