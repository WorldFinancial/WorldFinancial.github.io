  1. Zhou Hui, CASS: Responding to the risk challenges in the development of the metaverse in accordance with the law
    On April 2, Zhou Hui, deputy director of the Network and Information Law Research Office of the Institute of Law of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, published an article entitled “Responding to the risk challenges in the development of metaverse according to law”, which stated that since 2021, technology applications and industrial investments around metaverse have increasingly become a hot topic of social concern. Microsoft, Nvidia, Facebook and other foreign technology companies have already carried out the layout of metaverse ecology-related technology products. The topic of metaverse has become a new hotspot for the development of digital economy and the forefront of digital government applications. The metaverse has become a realistic application from a science fiction concept, and will form a virtual world that maps and interacts with the real world. At the same time, some unscrupulous elements may take the opportunity to use, bundle or even fictitiously create the concept of “metaverse” to engage in illegal and criminal activities. In this regard, it is necessary to correctly understand the development of metaverse, and at the same time, prudently study the various risks it brings, so as to lay out governance solutions in advance.

  1. Governor of Central Bank of Kenya: Low smartphone penetration is not conducive to launching CBDC’s plans
    Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) Governor Patrick Njoroge said that more than half of Kenya’s mobile phone users do not have access to smartphones, which is against its plans to launch the CBDC. Njoroge explained that CBDC will have minimum viable technology requirements, which could be a fourth generation (4G) environment. There is a view that such a development could lead to greater financial exclusion, to the extent that some people may drop out of the financial system because we have adopted CBDC, and that is something we need to be careful about. The governor suggested that CBK may have to wait until Kenya has more smartphone users, of the 59 million mobile devices used by Kenyans, about 56% or 33 million are non-smartphones or feature phones. Feature phones do not support the internet, which means that owners of these devices cannot use CBDC.

  2. US Senator introduces bill to prohibit the Federal Reserve from issuing CBDCs directly to individuals and competing with the private sector
    US Senator Ted Cruz has introduced legislation that would prohibit the Federal Reserve from issuing central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) directly to individuals and competing with the private sector. The legislation would prohibit the Fed from developing a direct-to-consumer CBDC that could be used by the federal government as a financial oversight tool. The senator argued that a central bank digital currency (CBDC) must adhere to three basic principles: protecting financial privacy, maintaining the dominance of the US dollar, and fostering innovation. A CBDC that fails to do so could allow entities like the Federal Reserve to mobilise themselves into retail banks, collect personally identifiable information from users and track their transactions indefinitely. The senator noted that unlike decentralised digital currencies like Bitcoin, which are issued and backed by government entities and traded on a centralised, permissioned blockchain, this CBDC model could not only centralise Americans’ financial information, making it vulnerable to attack, but could also be used as a direct monitoring tool for Americans’ private transactions.

  3. US Senator: From a policy-making perspective, owning bitcoin is no different than owning cattle
    US Senator Cynthia Lummis is preparing to introduce the Responsible Financial Innovation Act, which would affect the way cryptocurrencies are taxed. She says that from a policy-making perspective, owning bitcoin is no different from owning cattle. As a senator taking office in 2020, Lummis reported holding between $50,000 and $100,000 worth of BTC, and in August 2021 she reported buying an additional $50,000 to $100,000 worth of BTC. she did not receive any income from the sale of her assets.

  4. US Senator Warren compares buying Bitcoin to buying air
    US Senator Elizabeth Warren (D-MA) recently compared buying bitcoin to buying air on NBC host Chuck Todd’s show. Warren claimed that buying cryptocurrency is a bet on whether the token will become more valuable or less valuable. She also said that now is the right time for the US to issue a central bank digital currency. Bitcoin will not escape regulation, but she did not specify what specific rules should apply to Bitcoin.

5, the Federal Reserve monetary policy is under unprecedented tightening pressure
April 1 news, Golden 10 futures special Guotai Junan futures review of the U.S. non-farm payrolls data: this non-farm payrolls data portrayed the U.S. labor market still remains strong, while payroll pressure remains unabated, inflation expectations - payrolls spiral is gradually brewing. Combined with the US PCE price index for February released on March 31, up 6.4% year-on-year (the highest level since 1982), all signals indicate that inflation is developing in an increasingly aggressive direction, and also means that the Fed’s monetary policy is under unprecedented tightening pressure. Market expectations for a 50bp rate hike in May are currently at 77%, with expectations for a further 50bp hike in June at over 60%. The path of impact of the rate hike on gold may not be transmitted from the logical line of nominal interest rates going up and affecting real interest rates going up, but whether the Fed’s move will succeed in curbing inflation in the context of a soft economic landing or not. Only if the two conditions of a soft economic landing and a significant fall in inflation are met simultaneously could gold’s price centre of gravity move significantly lower.
