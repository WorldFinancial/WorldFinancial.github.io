2022 Warren Buffett’s shareholder meeting brings inspiration to ordinary people

Berkshire Hathaway’s 2022 Annual Meeting of Shareholders was held in Omaha, Nebraska, on April 30, with CEO Warren Buffett and Vice Chairman Charlie Munger in attendance. The event, known as the “Spring Gala of the investment world”, was held again after two years and won the attention of many people. The conference was divided into two sessions and lasted for nearly six hours.

According to reports, the number of attendees at this year’s conference may have exceeded 40,000, a record high. Due to the epidemic, the previous two years were broadcast live online, but the number of participants this time far exceeded expectations, highlighting the charisma of the “God of Stock” Warren Buffett on the one hand, and showing that value investing has proven to be an effective investment philosophy on the other.

The conference saw several corporate executives and other investors flock to the center of the event, with Buffett’s longtime friend Bill Gates, and Apple CEO Tim Cook also appearing. In addition, JPMorgan Chase CEO Dimon also participated in the conference, which was his first time attending a Buffett shareholder meeting.

Let’s take a look at Buffett’s and Munger’s views and advice on the stock market, young people and the current market!

  1. The importance of cash holdings
    In his opening comments, Buffett recalled the 2008 economic crisis, saying, “The one thing that never changes is that we always have a lot of cash on hand.”

Berkshire’s cash reserves fell to $106.3 billion in the first quarter from $144 billion at the end of 2021, but that’s still a lot of money.” For him, “cash is like oxygen.” Buffett also revealed that Berkshire Hathaway’s cash reserves are always ample and can be “better than banks” in providing lines of credit to companies in need.

“Cash is king” is Warren Buffett’s investment credo for more than half a century, Berkshire has a large amount of cash equivalents and bonds on its books every year, with a position close to 85% of the peak, mainly because when the opportunity comes Warren Buffett can take out a lot of cash to bet.

2、How to seize the investment time?
In the face of the ups and downs of the capital market, stock prices are always up and down, how to choose the right time is a great concern for most investors. In the exchange Warren Buffett said that he is not good at choosing the time, and he never figured out how to determine the timing of the market, at the same time, he also showed that he had missed many opportunities. For example, the U.S. stocks melted down several times at the beginning of the outbreak in March 2020, and the Dow sank 12.9% in a single day, the biggest one-day drop since 1987, but he did not effectively grasp it.

Buffett sticks to a strategy of value investing, advising people that they should not spend their time answering questions like how to beat the S&P 500 this year. “We’re not good at timing. What we’re good at is figuring out when we’ve got enough money for ourselves. We don’t know when we’re going to buy something, but we keep hoping that (the market) will go down for a while so we might buy more.”

3, against short-term speculation and gambling
Buffett and Munger, who adhere to the concept of value investing, were bitterly critical of short-term speculation in the market over the past two years at the conference. Buffett said: the past two years of stock market turmoil, like a casino, everyone is gambling in it. Munger also said at the conference that we have never been in such a crazy situation as we are now, gambling like crazy; buying quickly, selling quickly, and it really doesn’t look good. Some traders know nothing about the stock market, but they are being guided by much less experienced brokers.

  1. The best protection against inflation is to invest in yourself
    In response to a question about “how inflation affects Berkshire’s business,” Buffett said this round of inflation is very different from previous experience. In the company’s furniture stores, although the price of furniture is rising rapidly, but the rich people are still willing to pay for it, in earlier years, there are times when people do not have money in hand, even can not afford to buy candy.

Buffett said that although we want to understand how serious inflation is, and the future stage of development of inflation, but no one can be completely clear. The best way to protect yourself under the current inflation is to invest in yourself, such as playing the violin and other such talents. “No matter what happens, your talent is not going to run away and will protect you. Your money may be affected by inflation, but talent never will.”

5, Views on Bitcoin
Warren Buffett insists that Bitcoin does not generate any value. He stated that if you owned a farm or apartment in all of the United States and then wanted to sell me 1% of it, I would be happy to make the sale. Because farms are productive, apartments generate rent, and bitcoin is not such a productive asset, and its value is dependent on how much the underling pays the overling.

Munger says, “Throughout my life, I’ve tried to avoid something that was both stupid, evil, and made me look worse than everyone else, and I think Bitcoin has all three.” First bitcoin investment is stupid because it will probably go to zero; second bitcoin is evil because it undermines the Federal Reserve system, it undermines the national monetary system; and it makes us look bad that we’re still making all kinds of assumptions here compared to countries that have banned bitcoin. Munger said that when you have your own retirement account and your financial manager tells you to invest all your money in bitcoin, that’s when you say “no.”

6、How do you feel about investing in China
Warren Buffett said there is some real nervousness right now, and it’s affecting some of our Chinese stock prices, especially the prices of Chinese tech stocks.

Munger admitted in the Q&A that it is indeed a little more difficult to invest across borders and to make investments in China than in the United States. But he said the reason for investing in China is to be able to buy better companies there at a lower price.　

  1. To become a better person in the second half of life
    Buffett talked about his outlook on life, taking marriage as an example, people tend to hide their weaknesses before marriage and show a better version of themselves, while Charlie Munger added “it’s okay, people are going to improve”. Buffett believes that it is more important to be a better person in the second half of life than to show off your wealth.

8、Advice for young people
Someone asked: you know early on you want to be an investor, if you do not know what you want to do, you have any advice?

Warren Buffett said I want to be an investor, it so happens that my father is doing this and like this. I had enough books at home for me to read, which was a fluke. But I do think that finding out what you really want to do and doing it for the rest of your life, finding out what kind of world you want to live in, working for people you really respect, that’s what I did after I graduated. I wanted the world of (“the father of securities analysis”) Benjamin Graham. Then he did hire me, and then I worked for myself, and I just wanted to work for myself. Munger’s advice is to find the ones you’re really bad at and then circumvent them. Even if you’re smart, you can’t be good at something you’re not interested in.
