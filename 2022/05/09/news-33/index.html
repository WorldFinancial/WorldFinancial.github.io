How much is the performance difference between PCIe 3.0/4.0 and RX 6400 up to 35% in real-world testing?

|Post View:

AMD recently released its entry-level graphics card, the RX 6400, which, like the RX 6500 XT before it, only supports the PCIe 4.0 x4 system interface with a unidirectional bandwidth of 8GB/s, equivalent to PCIe 3.0 x8.

TechPowerUp did an interesting test to examine the performance of the RX 6400 in PCIe 4.0 x4 and PCIe 3.0 x4 modes for 25 games and 3 resolutions to see how much difference it could make.


Looking directly at the summary of results: in PCIe 3.0 mode, the RX 6400 loses 14% of its performance in both 1080p/2K resolution, and plummets 23% in 4K resolution.

Of course, for a card of this caliber, 2K/4K resolutions are of little consequence, but they do illustrate a trend.

The performance of different games is also very different. The PCIe 3.0 mode in “Assassin’s Creed: Valhalla” and “God of War” all lost as much as 35% of the performance, while “The Witcher 3” and so on are almost unchanged, the latter are mainly some old games.

In addition, for games that require large-capacity video memory, the RX 6400 4GB is easily insufficient. At this time, it is necessary to borrow PCIe channels and system memory transmission resources, and PCIe 3.0 will become a bottleneck.

TPU recommendation, if you are still using PCIe 3.0 system, such as Intel 10th generation Core or earlier, AMD first generation Ryzen, don’t consider RX 6400, it is not as good as the previous RX 570 4GB, which is not expensive and supports full PCIe 3.0 x16, the game performance can be better, sometimes up to 17% higher than the RX 6400.
