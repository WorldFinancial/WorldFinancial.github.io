A comprehensive explanation of how Music NFT empowers the creator economy

  1. Music NFT Overview

1.1 Industry Overview
The domestic music market has rebounded in the last year with the passing of the epidemic and the rise of short-form video platforms, and the China Musicians Report 2021, written by the Communication University of China, shows that the proportion of musicians earning lower and middle incomes in 2021 has decreased, while the proportion of musicians earning higher incomes has increased. In 2021, 48.51% of musicians will earn less than $5,000 per month, 28.65% will earn more than $10,000 per month, and 3.41% will earn more than $50,000 per month.

The report also shows that 64.75% of musicians have uploaded short music videos in 2021, compared to 54.08% in 2020, an increase of 10% year-on-year. Of these, more than half, 55.21%, have used the Jitterbug platform to distribute their music. The effectiveness of short video platforms in spreading music, especially Jitterbug, has been recognised by musicians. In the choice of the most effective digital music platform for promotion, 33.37% of musicians chose Jitterbug, ranking second only to Tencent Music.

In overseas music markets where streaming dominates, the profit margins for streaming are low for 99% of artists, low enough to be a deterrent.

“…… Achieving around 1 million UK streams per month on a consistent basis may be some sort of guide, a minimum threshold for making a living from music, at least if UK streaming is supplemented by non-UK streaming and other revenue streams ….. .the number of artists achieving 1 million UK streams or more in October 2020 is 1723 …… or 0.41% of all artists [in our database].”

Quoted in ‘Revenue for Music Creators in the Digital Age’, a 224 page report by the UK Intellectual Property Office (IPO)

After Defi Summer, the profile picture (PFP) avatar NFT circuit has surged like a thunderclap and music NFTs are the next focus of attention. However, the social and financial value generated by the music NFT series has yet to reach the same level of sustained success as their visual counterparts. Music NFTs will have a market value of approximately $1,243 million in 2021, compared to avatar NFTs with a market value of approximately $10.4 billion.

1.2 Technical Background
The vast majority of music NFTs are traded and circulated on OpenSea, and the platform’s own trading marketplace based on ZORA, an NFT marketplace protocol that is non-downtime, composable, immutable, and accessible. allows for a permissionless system.

ZORA has a built-in promoter incentive that allows promoters to find the ultimate buyer for the NFT. When an NFT item is issued, a share of the promoter incentive fee is set and to earn the promoter incentive, the user simply submits the wallet address in the user interface and the platform generates a link. If that promoter finds a buyer, he or she earns that incentive. This is a powerful incentive mechanism that allows developers and curators to integrate the ZORA protocol and directly capture the value they create.

  1. Product Description
  2. 1 Euterpe
    Project Description

Euterpe is the world’s first IP-driven copyright NFT platform with an embedded SocialFi ecosystem. The team is committed to creating a professional B2C marketplace for high quality IP NFT, free from copyright infringement and fraud. euterpe will also embed the SocialFi ecosystem, turning passive fans into active stakeholders who are incentivised to contribute to the community through their efforts to contribute to the community. In a metaverse where IP is everything, Euterpe started with music IP and then expanded into a wide range of IP including videos, games, books, concerts, celebrity peripherals and more.

The Team

Jerry Liu (CEO), received his PhD in Intellectual Property Law from Stanford University and is currently a Research Fellow at Stanford University’s Center for Internet and Society, former Director of the Center for Intellectual Property and Technology Law at the University of San Francisco, and legal advisor to IP giants such as Disney, Warner, Microsoft, IFPI and the Motion Picture Association of America.

Charles Cheng (CFO) is a graduate of Stanford Law School. Previously a partner at Amino Capital, he is a consultant with Alumni Ventures Group, a member of Stanford Angels & Entrepreneurs, and was named to Forbes 2021 Top 60 North American Chinese Elite last year.

Lincoln Deng (CTO), PhD in Scientific Computing from Stanford, former founder of technology company Sally Inc, Senior Architect at ProPlus and Senior Engineer at Synopsys.

Leon Xiu (CCO), prior to joining Euterpe, he was Director of Chinese at Tencent Music Entertainment Group (TME), Director of Music at ByteDance and Director of Rights at Sony Music Entertainment.

Woody Mu (Head of Strategic Partnerships) joins Euterpe from Alibaba, where he was Chief Representative of Alibaba’s Greater Entertainment Strategy Committee, International Manager of Warner Bros. Pictures, and teacher of Zhang Yimou and Chen Kaige.

Roland Vogl (Advisor), Executive Director of Codex, the Stanford Law Technology Incubator, and Executive Director of the Technology Law Program at Stanford Law School. co-founder of Vator, Inc. and SIPX, Inc.

The Euterpe team consists of blockchain innovators and IP experts from Stanford University, Google, Meta, TME, ByteDance, Alibaba Entertainment, Sony Music and Warner Bro.

Funding

The team has secured over $7 million in early stage funding in early 2022 from investors including Fenbushi Capital, Huobi Ventures, BingX Venture, HKICEx, UpHonest, Brightway Future, LD Capital, Titan Capital, and others. The institutional focus is relatively high.

Products

Euterpe was beta tested in the first quarter of 2021 and has partnered with over 30 record labels and thousands of songs. According to the official disclosure, for creators and copyright holders, the Euterpe platform casts and distributes NFTs for the rights of their works, and significantly increases the value of copyright through Stanford’s Nobel Prize-winning NFT auction, creating a channel for instant cash and expanding the reach of their works through SocialFi; for investors For investors, the platform offers a variety of ways to participate (SocialFi), to enjoy the work and promote it, while Participants will be rewarded by the platform. The platform will feature three types of SocialFi mining.

Play Mining

Users can choose to participate in the play-as-mine mechanism, where they will be rewarded with a certain number of passes based on the length of their play.

Sharing

Users who have downloaded a copy of a work will be given a link to it by the EUT community, either on the EUT community or on other media platforms such as Twitter, Facebook or Telegram, and if other users download or watch the stream through this link, they will be rewarded with a Token.

Vote Mining

Each week the EUT community publishes a series of rankings, aggregating the most valuable works in different genres (e.g. the Top 100), which are voted on by artists, fans and other users in the community, combining usage dimensions to determine the final ranking.
