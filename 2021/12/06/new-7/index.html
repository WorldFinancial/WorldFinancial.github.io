Hainan, China： Virtual currency mining will be eliminated, with a price increase of 8 yuan per kilowatt hour

National and local governments are increasingly cracking down on virtual currency mining. Recently, Hainan Development and Reform Commission is directly listed as the elimination of the industry, electricity will also increase prices.

Hainan has decided to list virtual currency mining as an industry to be eliminated in order to clean up virtual currency mining and promote energy conservation and emission reduction, according to the notice. It will implement differential electricity prices with a price increase of 0.80 yuan per kilowatt-hour.

The notice asked Hainan Power Grid to ensure strict implementation of the differential pricing policy, and charge users of virtual currency “mining” in full and timely manner.

The circular also stressed that users who directly participate in electricity market transactions should be screened, and users of virtual currency “mining” should not be allowed to directly participate in electricity market transactions or enjoy electricity market profits in any way.

Users of virtual currency “mining” who have directly participated in electricity market transactions must quit within a specified time limit and purchase power on behalf of power grid enterprises, and the price is not 1.5 times that of other users.
