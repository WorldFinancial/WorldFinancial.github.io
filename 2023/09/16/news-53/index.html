GLOBAL MARKETS: EU ban on Ukrainian food imports expires, Biden shouts at auto giants to raise offers

|Post View:

Would a U.S. auto worker strike have a huge impact on the U.S. economy and society, and does it bring American society crashing down

As the market generally expected, in this year’s third “four witching day”, superimposed on the U.S. auto industry strike and the U.S. government is again close to the “shutdown” moment and other shortcomings, the U.S. stock market throughout the day to open low, the three major indexes all closed down more than 1%. And because of Friday’s significant decline, the Nasdaq and S&P 500 index weekly level also from up to down.

Over the next week, the Federal Reserve’s September economic forecast is the most closely watched piece of economic data. Meanwhile, markets are also nervously assessing the impact of the auto industry’s general strike, Ian Lyngen, head of U.S. interest rate strategy at BMO Capital Markets, said the Fed is unlikely to react because of a strike in one industry, but the job market’s rising pay offer is a factor that could keep U.S. policy rates at high levels for longer.

In terms of commodity markets, oil prices continue to strengthen posture, the market has been analysts began to look forward to the price of oil back to 100 U.S. dollars per barrel. In addition, the international gold price is also affected by the U.S. inflation expectations higher, but the cumulative volatility of this week is not large.

Market News

[U.S. President Joe Biden Speaks on Auto Industry Strike

As the U.S. auto industry strike officially began, Biden also delivered a brief speech on Friday, expressing support for workers in the auto industry. Biden began his remarks by saying, “Including over the past several years, America’s automakers have been setting record profits, and the reason behind that is the skill and sacrifice of members of the United Auto Workers (UAW). In my opinion, these record profits have not been shared fairly.”

Biden also expressed his gratitude to the representatives of all parties involved in the negotiations and called on the American carmakers to provide a better offer because “record profits should be shared with record contracts.”

[Indonesian President: Indonesia will have the largest electric vehicle battery production plant in Southeast Asia]

Indonesian President Joko said on Sept. 14 that the Hyundai and LG joint venture plant in the town of Garawang, West Java province, will be the largest electric vehicle battery production plant in Southeast Asia. The plant, operated by HLI Green Power, a joint venture between Hyundai and LG, is expected to go into operation early next year. During a tour of the plant, Joko said, “The capacity will be 30 million bio-batteries to meet the demand of about 180,000 electric vehicles.” Indonesia’s Investment Minister Bachriel said, “The production process is currently under testing and actual production will start in March next year. Upon completion, the plant will have an installed capacity of 30 gigawatts per year.

[EU’s Ukraine grain import ban expires

On Friday, local time, the European Union announced that it would not extend the ban on Ukrainian grain imports from five countries in Eastern Europe. The EU said it had concluded through its investigations that the market distortions in the five Eastern European countries had disappeared as a result of the work of the harmonization platform and the ban on food imports. However, a number of Eastern European countries, including Poland, have indicated that they will implement import bans on Ukrainian grain individually.

[Shipping giant Maersk: Global trade shows initial signs of rebound]

Shipping giant Maersk Group Chief Executive Officer Mr. Ko Wensheng said that global trade has shown initial signs of rebound, and the economic outlook for next year is also relatively optimistic.

Ke Wensheng pointed out in a media interview this week, “Unless there are some unexpected negative conditions, we expect that going into 2024, global trade will slowly rebound, this rebound will not be as prosperous as in the past few years, but certainly …… demand is more in line with the kind of pattern that we have seen on the consumer side. There won’t be as much inventory adjustment.”

[iPhone 15 series on sale in many countries Pro Max expected delivery time has been scheduled to November]

Beijing time on Friday evening, Apple officially launched the iPhone 15 series of cell phone pre-sale in all channels. From the preliminary data, the most updated iPhone 15 Pro Max model is the most sought after. On Apple’s U.S. official website, the pre-order arrival time for logistics channels has been pushed to mid-November, while shipments from China’s official website have also been scheduled for late October to early November. Similarly in markets such as the UK, Canada, and India, Pro Max wait times for shipment have reached six to eight weeks.

In addition to consumers’ preference for the most updated models, the lack of stock availability could also be the reason for the long shipping times. Caixin reported at the end of July that the iPhone 15 Pro and iPhone 15 Pro Max displays were experiencing difficulties in manufacturing, which may have led to a limited number of devices for both models when they hit the market in September.

[U.S. private equity firm 777 Partners to buy English Premier League club Everton]

English Premier League club Everton released a statement on Sept. 15 saying that U.S. private equity firm 777 Partners has signed an agreement with team owner Farhad Moshiri to acquire all 94.1 percent of its shares. The deal is expected to be completed by the end of this year. 777 Partners owns a number of soccer clubs, including Serie A’s Genoa and Belgium’s Liège, as well as shares in La Liga’s Sevilla and Australia’s Melbourne Victory.
