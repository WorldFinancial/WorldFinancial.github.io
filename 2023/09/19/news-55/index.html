India's G20 summit a success U.S. finding more effective ways to deal with China?

Created2023-09-19|Updated2023-09-19|Internet
|Post View:

Who will win the war between China and the U.S.
What the absence of China and the U.S. from the G20 summit for the first time foreshadows?

The U.S.-China trade issue has been going on for years, and with the G20 meeting, could it be possible for the U.S. to turn things around?

Chinese President Xi Jinping’s absence from the Group of 20 (G20) summit last weekend (Sept. 9 and 10) may have been an attempt to give the G20 presidency, India, the cold shoulder. But Bloomberg says Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi, as well as U.S. and European allies, have found ways to more effectively counter China on the world stage.

G20 members recognized India’s successful bid to get the G20 joint statement adopted, although days before the summit, question marks remained over whether a joint G20 statement could be released. The summit not only agreed on the thorniest issue of the Russia-Ukraine war, but also adopted the African Union as a full member of the G20, and the joint statement also covered emerging market priorities such as climate change and debt sustainability.

The outcome of the G20 summit in New Delhi angered Ukraine, with Kiev arguing that the G20 joint statement’s language on the Russia-Ukraine war was weaker than that of the joint statement from last year’s G20 summit in Bali, Indonesia.

But for the U.S. and its allies, the criticism of the New Delhi G20 joint statement was a small price to pay to allow Modi to win this round and elevate India’s status as a rising power in order to weaken China’s global influence.

U.S. President Joe Biden, who spearheaded the initiative, sees India as the best hope for isolating China and Russia, and also as a shot in the arm for a world order dominated by the U.S., the report said.
